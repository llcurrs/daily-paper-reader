## Abstract 

While multimodal integration significantly improves com-puter vision models, deploying them incurs prohibitive in-ference costs and requires scarce, perfectly paired datasets. Recent methods address this data bottleneck by synthesizing missing modalities via generative AI, yet they introduce a severe inefficiency: the Decode-Encode Loop. Specifically, information-rich generative latents are decoded into noisy raw signals, forcing the downstream classifier to waste ca-pacity re-encoding them. To bypass this bottleneck, we propose Direct Latent Augmentation (DLA), utilizing un-decoded generative latents directly as privileged informa-tion. Furthermore, to transfer this dense knowledge to a purely visual student, we introduce Multilayer Explicit Sim-ulated Synesthesia (MESSy). Instead of enforcing rigid rep-resentation matching, which forces the student to distort its native visual features to accommodate complex multimodal topologies, MESSy uses a predictive objective to safely in-ternalize these physical priors. Empirical results demon-strate that our framework significantly outperforms raw data augmentation and traditional distillation. Ultimately, our approach yields highly accurate unimodal students with “synesthetic” latent structures that are inherently aligned with physical properties they have never directly observed. 

## 1. Introduction 

Multimodal architectures, fusing 2D images with addi-tional modalities, consistently outperform unimodal mod-els across multiple domains. However, processing multiple high-dimensional streams incurs substantial inference costs, and auxiliary modalities are rarely available in the wild. While cross-modal knowledge distillation solves the infer-ence bottleneck by transferring a heavy teacher’s knowl-edge to an image-only student, a paradigm known as Learn-ing Using Privileged Information (LUPI), it fundamentally relies on scarce, perfectly paired multimodal datasets. Generative AI can overcome this data scarcity by syn-thesizing missing modalities, an approach recently formal-ized as Learning Using Generated Privileged Information (LUGPI). Yet, current pipelines overlook a key structural feature: they force semantically dense generative latents through an expensive decoding step to produce “raw” data.

This creates a highly inefficient Decode-Encode Loop , in-jecting generative artifacts and perceptual noise that the downstream classifier must waste capacity re-encoding. We propose Direct Latent Augmentation (DLA) to by-pass this inefficiency, directly utilizing undecoded gener-ative latents as pristine privileged information. However, transferring this dense knowledge to a unimodal student presents a challenge. Alongside standard logit-level knowl-edge distillation (KD), existing cross-modal methods typ-ically supplement training by minimizing the direct Eu-clidean ( L2) distance between embedding spaces. This im-poses a rigid geometric isomorphism causing capacity in-terference , where forcing a visual student to mimic high-dimensional audio and 3D spaces degrades its native dis-criminative ability. To resolve this, we introduce Multilayer Explicit Simulated Synesthesia (MESSy). Rather than iden-tically matching spaces, MESSy employs lightweight pre-dictive heads, allowing the student to predict the teacher’s auxiliary features from its own visual tokens. This ensures the student internalizes physical priors without collapsing its native visual manifold. 

## 2. Prior Work. 

Traditional Learning Using Privileged Information (LUPI) [17] bridges multimodal training and unimodal inference via cross-modal distillation [6, 7], but it fundamentally re-lies on perfectly aligned real-world datasets that are expen-sive to scale. Recent generative augmentation pipelines, such as Learning Using Generated Privileged Information (LUGPI) [9], circumvent this by synthesizing auxiliary data. However, they operate exclusively in the raw data do-main (text and image). This not only ignores critical physi-cal modalities like audio and 3D, but the decoding step also injects perceptual noise that wastes downstream parame-ter capacity. Finally, to stabilize optimization, feature-level distillation often aligns multiple hidden layers [12, 13, 16]. In cross-modal LUPI, this is typically enforced by rigidly minimizing the L2 distance between embeddings. We demonstrate that in asymmetric architectures, this strict ge-ometric isomorphism induces capacity interference , limit-ing the visual student from fully exploiting the cross-modal distillation and motivating our predictive MESSy objective.

Decoding D in-jects perceptual noise that degrades the semantic signal. To avoid this and fully leverage synthetic signals, we intercept the generative pipeline to extract latents directly. Given an RGB image query Xv , we extract 3D and audio latents without synthesizing raw signals (Phase 1, Fig. 2). For 3D, we employ Hunyuan3D-2 Latent DiT [15] to obtain continuous latents Z3D ∈ RN3D ×D3D . For audio, we use Gemma3 [14] to create acoustic captions, strictly filtering out class names, and pass this to Audiocraft [1], extracting undecoded discrete codes Zaud ∈ ZNaud . We report full generation details in the supplementary material. To process these heterogeneous latents (Phase 2, Fig. 2), we design a Multimodal Teacher Network T . Standard image patches Ev are fused with Z3D and Zaud using modality-specific Perceiver cross-attention blocks, which compress the variable-length latents into a fixed budget of resampled tokens ( E3D , Eaud ). MESSy Distillation In the final phase, our objective is to transfer the multimodal representations of the frozen Teacher T into a lightweight, purely visual Student S. As discussed, standard cross-modal distillation enforces a strict geometric isomorphism (minimizing the L2 distance be-tween embeddings). This forces the lower-capacity student to distort its optimal visual decision boundaries to geomet-rically accommodate the missing modalities, inducing de-structive capacity interference [18]. To resolve this bottleneck, we propose Multilayer Ex-plicit Simulated Synesthesia (MESSy) (Phase 3 of Fig. 2). Rather than enforcing rigid L2 spatial equality, MESSy re-quires the student to encode only the sufficient statistics necessary to reconstruct the auxiliary modalities. 4.1. MESSy Distillation Efficacy 

We evaluate transferring our robust multimodal knowledge to a unimodal Student ( S) in Table 3. Our Latent-Distilled Student (MESSy) achieves the best unimodal performance, outperforming the image-only baseline by +8 .42% on Cal-tech101 and +6 .97% on ImageNet-100. ## 5. Conclusion 

We introduced Direct Latent Augmentation (DLA) to by-pass the inefficient decode-encode loop of generative mul-timodal training, utilizing pristine latents directly as privi-leged information. To seamlessly transfer this dense knowl-edge, our MESSy distillation objective allows a purely vi-sual student to internalize acoustic and geometric priors without suffering from capacity interference.
